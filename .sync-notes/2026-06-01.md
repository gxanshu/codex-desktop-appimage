## Per-file conflict reasoning

### Cargo.toml — workspace members

- Before sync: `members = ["computer-use-linux"]` only.
- After sync: `members = ["computer-use-linux", "read-aloud-linux"]`.
- Reasoning: the sync rules in the runbook (and the slim-invariant
  check it sets) require both crates in the workspace. `read-aloud-linux/`
  already exists in the tree with its own `Cargo.toml` but was not built
  because it was missing from the workspace. Adding it back makes
  `cargo check --workspace` actually compile it. `updater` stays
  excluded.

### Makefile

- Took ours (the fork's slim, AppImage-only Makefile).
- Upstream added `MAX_BUILD_THREADS` plumbing and several deb/rpm/pacman
  targets. Neither is needed for the fork: `MAX_BUILD_THREADS=0` is the
  upstream default anyway, and the package targets call deleted scripts.

### .github/workflows/ci.yml

- Took ours but adopted upstream's `permissions: pull-requests: read`
  addition, per the runbook.
- Upstream's new `package-deb`, `nix`, `package-rpm`, `package-pacman`
  jobs all call deleted scripts; they were dropped.
- Upstream's `CI_PACKAGE_VERSION` env var is deb/rpm/pacman-specific and
  not used by the AppImage build.

### README.md

- Took ours (the fork's AppImage-only narrative).
